Participation

Drag a slider or type each investor's commitment. Tick Oversubscribe and an investor automatically absorbs a share of the round's shortfall (weighted by holding, up to an optional cap) — shrinking or removing the External Party.

Result table

Final ownership for every shareholder after the round.

How the model works: Shares are issued at a fixed pre-money price per share = Pre-money ÷ Existing Shares. Each investor receives (Actual Investment ÷ Price) new shares. Final ownership = (current + new shares) ÷ total shares after the round — the denominator grows as money comes in, so ownership does not move linearly with the cheque size. When investors oversubscribe, they absorb the shortfall left by non-participants first — split pro-rata to their current holding and capped at any limit you set — and only the remainder spills to the External Party.
