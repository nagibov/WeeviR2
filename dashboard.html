How the model works

Shares are issued at a fixed pre-money price per share = Pre-money ÷ Existing Shares. Each investor receives (Actual Investment ÷ Price) new shares. Final ownership = (current + new shares) ÷ total shares after the round — the denominator grows as money comes in, so ownership does not move linearly with the cheque size. When investors cover the gap, they absorb the shortfall left by non-participants first — split pro-rata to their current holding and capped at any limit you set — and only the remainder spills to the External Party.

Source model: Docs/Shares Dashboard.xlsx → "Dashboard" sheet (Option 4 · Pro-Rata + Oversubscription). Built for the Round 2 investor meeting.
